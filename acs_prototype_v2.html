Apex Combat Series returned to Bradford for ACS 23 on Valentine's Day 2026, delivering a night of high-octane finishes at the Bradford Live Arena. The main event saw Darius Kane cement his status as one of the UK's most dominant lightweights, stopping Marcos Vidal inside the opening round with a crushing right hand to retain the ACS Lightweight Championship.

Seven of eight bouts ended before the scorecards. Luca Ferreira continued his impressive run with a second-round submission of former title challenger Pedro Esteves, and Jake Novak reversed his ACS 20 loss with a second-round KO of Sam Bashir. Danny Cross and Rafael Branco both delivered swift submission finishes on the undercard, while Tariq Malik took the night's only decision.

ACS 24 Fight Card Announced:
Eight Fighters, One Prize

The Featherweight Tournament bracket is confirmed for 23 August at Indigo at The O2.

Apex Combat Series has confirmed the full bracket for ACS 24: Winner Takes All — the promotion's most ambitious single-night event to date. Eight featherweights will compete on 23 August 2026 at Indigo at The O2 in London for a £10,000 prize, the largest single-fight purse in the promotion's history.

The Format

The tournament runs over a single evening: four quarter-finals, two semi-finals and a grand final, all contested at featherweight. Every fighter enters on equal footing. The bracket will be confirmed closer to fight night. In addition to the tournament, Luca Ferreira (9–2–0) faces Marcus Webb (11–3–1) in a lightweight co-main event — two of the promotion's most recognised names squaring off outside the tournament format.

The Prize

The £10,000 winner-takes-all prize represents a landmark moment for UK MMA. Tickets are on sale now through AXS, and the event will be streamed live on the Apex Combat Series PPV platform at apexcombatseries.tv.

Fighter Focus

Darius Kane:
Back-to-Back & Beyond

The ACS Lightweight Champion reflects on his title defences and sets his sights higher.

Darius Kane doesn't do close fights. In two Apex Combat Series title appearances — ACS 21 against Pedro Esteves and ACS 23 against Marcos Vidal — the lightweight champion has needed a combined five minutes and three seconds. Both ended by KO in the first round. Both sent the Bradford crowd to its feet.

On the Title Defences

Speaking after ACS 23, Kane was measured. "I came in prepared. The game plan was to be first and be accurate. When the opportunity came, I took it. That's what champions do." The 2:16 stoppage of Vidal was clinical — a right hand that put him down and prompted an immediate stoppage.

What's Next

With two title fights in four months and no signs of slowing, the question around Apex Combat Series is no longer whether Kane is the best lightweight on the roster — it's whether the division can produce a credible challenger. A potential third title defence or a crossover to featherweight are both in conversation.

ACS 23 Recap:
Kane Defends, Ferreira Impresses, Bashir Falls

Everything that happened on Valentine's Day in Bradford.

Bradford's Live Arena delivered a ferocious Valentine's Day card as Apex Combat Series returned to the north. Seven of eight bouts ended before the scorecards, with the main event setting the tone inside the opening three minutes.

Kane Defends in Style

Darius Kane made his second successive first-round stoppage in title competition, catching Marcos Vidal with a straight right hand at 2:16 of round one. The finishing sequence was emphatic — Vidal never recovered from the initial shot, and referee intervention was immediate.

Ferreira Rolls On

Luca Ferreira's (9–2–0) submission sequence continued at ACS 23. A second-round head and arm triangle locked in against Pedro Esteves was the latest addition to a growing highlight reel. Ferreira is the standout name for the ACS 24 co-main event, and his grappling record inside the ACS cage is becoming impossible to ignore.

Bashir's Reversal

Sam Bashir's defeat to Jake Novak was the result that will sting longest. A rematch of their ACS 20 contest, Novak landed a second-round TKO to reverse the earlier result. Both men now sit level — the trilogy is inevitable.
